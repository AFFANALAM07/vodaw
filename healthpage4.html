Medical Doctors in East Asia

Introduction-:
The healthcare workforce, especially medical doctors, is a vital component in assessing the quality and accessibility of healthcare systems. East Asia, a region characterized by rapid economic growth and technological advancements, has seen significant progress in its healthcare sector. This report delves into the trends in the number of medical doctors and their ratios per 10,000 population i n East Asia from 2000 to 2022, with a focus on five countries: China, Japan, South Korea (Republic of Korea), North Korea (Democratic People's Republic of Korea), a nd Mongolia. It also highlights key insights derived from interactive visualizations and slicer functionality.

Dashboard-:
The visualization provides an interactive and comprehensive perspective on the trends in East Asia's healthcare workforce:
Line Graphs:Depict the year-by-year growth in the number of physicians for each country, offering a clear view of the progress over two decades.
Bar Graphs: Highlight physicians per 10,000 population, enabling easy comparison of healthcare accessibility across countries.
Country-Specific Insights:Users can explore trends by focusing on individual nations, helping identify disparities and progress within the region.

Trends in Medical Doctors
Growth in Absolute Numbers:
1) China: Witnessed a remarkable increase in its medical workforce, starting from 1.6 million doctors in 2000 and reaching 4.4 million by 2022. This growth mirrors China's population and healthcare reforms aimed at improving accessibility.
2) Japan: Maintained steady growth, increasing from approximately 245,300 doctors in 2000 to over 331,100 in 2022, reflecting a focus on maintaining high standards in healthcare delivery.
3) South Korea: Saw consistent increases, growing from 60,900 doctors in 2000 to over 130,000 by 2022, indicating significant investment in medical education and healthcare infrastructure.
4) North Korea: Despite challenges, the number of medical doctors improved modestly over the period, showcasing efforts to sustain its healthcare system under difficult circumstances.
5) Mongolia: Though relatively smaller in absolute numbers, Mongolia’s steady growth from 6,000 to 12,000 doctors reflects substantial progress in addressing healthcare needs in a sparsely populated country.

Physicians per 10,000 Population:
1) China: Progressed from 12.6 doctors per 10,000 in 2000 to 25.2 in 2022, showing marked improvements in healthcare accessibility.
2) Japan: Maintained one of the highest ratios in the region, starting at 19.3 in 2000 and reaching 26.5 by 2022, consistent with its reputation for a robust healthcare system.
3) South Korea: Recorded an impressive rise from 13 in 2000 to 26.1 in 2022, underscoring the country's advancements in healthcare quality and delivery.
4) North Korea: Showed slower growth, with figures increasing modestly but remaining below regional averages.
5) Mongolia: Demonstrated a notable rise, moving from 26.5 in 2000 to 41.3 in 2022, reflecting focused efforts to enhance healthcare services in a smaller population.

Conclusion -:
East Asia has made significant progress in strengthening its healthcare workforce, with countries like China, Japan, and South Korea leading the way in absolute numbers and ratios. However, disparities persist, particularly in North Korea and Mongolia, highlighting the need for continued investment and policy focus. These trends underscore the importance of balanced growth to ensure equitable healthcare access across the region.
