Food and Vitamins: Their Role in Human Health

Introduction:-
A balanced diet rich in essential nutrients is fundamental to maintaining good health and preventing deficiencies. Vitamins and minerals play crucial roles in various bodily functions, including immunity, metabolism, cell repair, and overall well-being. This report explores the classification of dietary components, the sources of key vitamins, their recommended intake, and their physiological roles.

Dietary Classifications and Main Sources :-
The primary dietary components contributing to vitamin intake include:
1)Vitamins :
• Sources: Eggs, Fish, Fresh Fruits, Vegetables, Meat, Dairy, Nuts, Whole Cereals, Yeast
• Includes multiple vitamins such as A, B-complex, C, D, E, and K.
• Essential for immunity, vision, skin health, and cell repair.
• Supports enzymatic functions and overall metabolism.

2)Proteins :
• Sources: Eggs, Fish, Meat, Dairy, Nuts, Pulses, Whole Cereals, Potatoes
• Rich in B-complex vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B6, B12) and Vitamin D.
• Vitamins B support energy metabolism, red blood cell formation, and muscle growth.

3)Minerals :
• Sources: Water, Milk, Juices, Other Beverages
• Contains Vitamin C, Vitamin D, B2, and small amounts of Vitamin K.
• Supports hydration, digestion, blood circulation, and immune health.

4)Liquids :
• Sources: Cereals, Eggs, Fish, Fruits, Meat, Dairy, Nuts, Salt, Vegetables, Yeast
• Provides essential minerals like Calcium, Iron, Magnesium, Zinc, and Phosphorus.
• Supports enzyme function, bone health, and cardiovascular regulation.

Recommended Vitamin Intake :-
The required vitamin intake varies by gender and dietary needs:
For Men:
B1: 1.2 mg, B2: 1.3 mg, B3: 16 mg, B6: 1.3 mg, B12: 2.4 mcg, C: 90 mg, D: 15 mcg, K: 120 mcg.
For Women:
B1: 1.1 mg, B2: 1.1 mg, B3: 14 mg, B6: 1.3 mg, B12: 2.4 mcg, C: 75 mg, D: 15 mcg, K: 90 mcg.

Conclusion :-
A well-balanced diet ensures optimal vitamin and mineral intake, reducing the risk of deficiencies and supporting overall health. The visualization serves as a valuable tool for understanding dietary choices and their impact on wellness.

3). Further Studies: Explore how enzyme supplementation can aid individuals with metabolic deficiencies.
