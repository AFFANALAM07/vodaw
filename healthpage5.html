Medical Doctors in West Asia

Introduction-:
The availability of medical doctors is a crucial factor in determining the efficiency and accessibility of healthcare systems. West Asia, a region with diverse economies and healthcare infrastructures, has experienced substantial changes in the number of physicians and their distribution relative to the population. This report examines the trends in the number of medical doctors and the ratio of doctors per 10,000 people across multiple countries in West Asia from 2000 to 2023. The interactive visualization and slicer functionality provide an in-depth exploration of these trends, helping policymakers and researchers analyze disparities and progress in the healthcare sector.

Dashboard-:
The visualization offers a detailed, interactive representation of physician trends in West Asia:
Line Graphs: Depicting the year-on-year increase in medical doctors across different countries.
Bar Graphs: Comparing the physician-to-population ratios, showcasing regional disparities.
Interactive Slicers:Allowing users to filter data by country and year to focus on specific trends and comparisons..

Trends in the no. of Medical Doctors
1) Türkiye: The number of medical doctors has consistently increased, reaching approximately 194,700 by 2023. The steady growth reflects investments in medical education and healthcare expansion.
2) Saudi Arabia: Grew from approximately 45,900 doctors in 2000 to over 113,300 in 2023.Significant investments in healthcare infrastructure contributed to this rise.
3) Iraq: Despite political instability, the number of physicians increased from 19,700 in 2000 to 30,100 in 2023. The country still faces challenges in retaining healthcare professionals due to migration and security concerns.
4) United Arab Emirates (UAE): One of the fastest-growing healthcare sectors, reaching 50.8 doctors per 10,000 people in 2023. The UAE has become a regional hub for medical services, attracting professionals worldwide.
5) Yemen: Struggles with low physician availability, with numbers fluctuating between 6,400 and 8,700 in the past decade. Ongoing conflicts have significantly impacted healthcare services
6) Lebanon, Israel, and Jordan: Lebanon and Israel maintain some of the highest physician-to-population ratios in the region, with Israel reaching 38 doctors per 10,000 people by 2023. Jordan has seen steady growth, improving its ratio to approximately 29 per 10,000.

Physicians per 10,000 Population:
1) High Ratio: Israel, UAE, and Lebanon lead with more than 35 physicians per 10,000 people, reflecting well-developed healthcare systems.
2) Moderate Ratios: Saudi Arabia, Jordan, and Türkiye maintain ratios between 22 and 30, showing substantial progress.
3) Low Ratios: Iraq, Yemen, and parts of the Palestinian territories have struggled to increase their physician density, with figures below 10 in some cases.
Conclusion -:
The data highlights both progress and disparities in physician availability across West Asia. While countries like Israel, UAE, and Saudi Arabia continue to expand their medical workforce, others like Yemen and Iraq face challenges due to political and economic instability. Understanding these trends is essential for designing policies that ensure equitable healthcare access and sustainable workforce growth.
