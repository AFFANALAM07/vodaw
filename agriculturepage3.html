Analysis of US Trade Trends of Food Items in Tons (Imports and Exports)

Introduction -:
This report provides an overview of the trends in United States imports and exports over several decades. The data spans a wide range of commodities, including agricultural products, oils, and processed goods. By analyzing the data, key patterns in trade volumes and the underlying factors driving these trends are explored.

Key Commodities in US Trade -:
The dataset highlights significant commodities in both import and export categories:
Imports: Major imported items include raw abaca (manila hemp), almonds (in-shell and shelled), animal oils and fats, and various fruits such as apples and apricots. These products reflect the demand for both raw materials and exotic agricultural goods within the US.
Exports: Exported items mirror similar categories, including almonds, apple juice, and animal fats, indicating a strong domestic production base for these goods and their popularity in international markets.

Trends Observed -:
Volume Growth Over Time:
Both import and export volumes have shown a consistent growth trend over the decades.Imports peaked at over 2 million tons annually by 2021, while exports reached approximately 1 million tons in the same period, demonstrating a strong international trade presence.
Notable Variations by Commodity:
Almonds: A standout commodity with both shelled and in-shell almonds appearing prominently in import and export categories. This reflects the dual role of the US as a producer and consumer of almonds.
Fruits: Products like apples and apricots have maintained steady demand, with exports often bolstered by surplus domestic production. Seasonality and Economic Impact:
Fluctuations in trade volumes can be attributed to seasonal agricultural cycles and global economic conditions.Periods of economic growth or policy changes, such as trade agreements, have significantly influenced these trends.

Dashboard -:
This Dashboard showcases the trends in US imports and exports (in tons) for various commodities, such as raw abaca, almonds, and apple juice, from the 1960s to 2022. Line graphs illustrate the fluctuations in trade volumes over the years, enabling a comparative analysis of imports and exports for each commodity. An interactive slicer is included, allowing users to filter the data by year, facilitating focused and detailed trend analysis for specific time periods.

Insights and Implications -:
Dependence on Specific Commodities: The heavy reliance on a few agricultural goods for exports may expose the US to risks from global market volatility and climate change.
Opportunities for Diversification: Expanding the portfolio of exportable goods, particularly in value-added processed products, can enhance trade resilience.
Sustainability in Trade: Sustainable sourcing and production practices could improve the marketability of US goods internationally, especially in environmentally conscious markets.

Conclusion -:
The US has demonstrated robust growth in both import and export activities over the years, with a strong emphasis on agricultural commodities. However, diversification and sustainable practices are crucial for maintaining competitiveness and addressing global challenges. Future analyses could delve deeper into the economic, environmental, and policy factors influencing these trends to provide actionable insights for stakeholders.

Recommendations -:
Conduct detailed studies on emerging markets for agricultural exports.
Invest in technologies that enhance the production and processing of key export goods. Promote trade policies that open new markets while ensuring sustainability and ethical sourcing.
