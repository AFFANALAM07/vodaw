The Earth's Atmosphere: Structure and Characteristics

Introduction:-
The Earth's atmosphere is a multilayered gaseous envelope surrounding the planet, essential for supporting life and regulating climate. It consists of distinct layers, each with unique characteristics influencing weather, radiation absorption, and space interactions. This report explores the five primary atmospheric layers: Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Thermosphere, and Exosphere, highlighting their composition, temperature trends, and key functions.

Atmospheric Layers and Their Features :-
1)Troposphere (0 - 12 km)
• Temperature Trend: Decreases with altitude.
• Pressure Range: ~101,325 Pa (lower) to ~20,000 Pa (upper).
• Composition: 78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen, 1% other gases (Argon, CO₂, etc.).
Key Features: Contains most atmospheric mass, weather patterns, and clouds.

2)Stratosphere (12 - 50 km)
• Temperature Trend: Increases with altitude due to ozone absorption of UV radiation.
• Pressure Range: ~20,000 Pa (lower) to ~1,000 Pa (upper).
• Composition: igh concentration of ozone (O₃) with standard atmospheric gases.
Key Features: Houses the ozone layer, absorbs harmful UV radiation, and is where commercial airplanes fly.

3)Mesosphere (50 - 85 km)
• Temperature Trend: Decreases with altitude, making it the coldest atmospheric layer.
• Pressure Range: ~1,000 Pa (lower) to ~0.01 Pa (upper).
• Composition: Contains some CO₂ and trace gases.
Key Features: Protects Earth by burning up meteors before they reach the surface.

4)Thermosphere (85 - 600 km)
• Temperature Trend: Increases with altitude due to solar radiation absorption.
• Pressure Range: ~0.01 Pa (lower) to ~10⁻⁶ Pa (upper).
• Composition: Mostly ionized particles.
Key Features: Hosts the ionosphere, high temperatures, and the orbit of the International Space Station (ISS).

5)Exosphere (600 km and beyond)
• Temperature Trend: Varies, gradually merging with space
• Pressure Range: Extremely low (<10⁻⁶ Pa).
• Composition: Dominantly hydrogen.
Key Features: Very thin air, where satellites orbit and space travel begins.
