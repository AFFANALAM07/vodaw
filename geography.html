Data Visualizations Related to Geography

Explore a diverse array of data visualizations focused on geographic trends, environmental policies, and regional systems across the globe. From global population distributions to region-specific environmental changes, we bring geographic data to life through interactive and visually engaging displays, making complex information accessible and easy to comprehend for a deeper understanding of the world around us.

Lakes of India

The visualization showcases India's major lakes, ranked by surface area, with Vembanad Lake (2,033 km²) being the largest. It allows for a detailed comparison of lake sizes across various regions. Additionally, selecting a specific lake provides an interactive feature, automatically directing you to its exact location via a satellite map.

Rivers in India

The visualization lists the major rivers in India, ranked by length, with the Indus River (3,180 km) being the longest. It provides a comparative overview of river lengths, including notable rivers such as the Brahmaputra (2,900 km), Ganga (2,525 km). This helps in understanding the geographical span and significance of India’s rivers.

Lowest Points on Surface of Earth and below the Sea

The visualization highlights the lowest points on Earth's surface and below sea level, such as the Dead Sea (-423 meters) and Mariana Trench (-10,902 meters). It compares these locations based on their depth.

Highest Mountain in Each Continent

The visualization highlights the Highest Mountain Peaks in every Continent With a Map Chart to Show Exact Location And A bar Chart To Compare height and A slicer to Filter Out A Specific Peak.
