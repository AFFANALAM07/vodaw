Major Wind Systems Globally

Introduction -:
 The dynamics of wind systems around the globe play a pivotal role in shaping weather patterns, influencing ocean currents, and impacting human settlements, agriculture, and climate. The report titled "Major Wind Systems Globally" offers a comprehensive classification of various wind systems categorized by their type (permanent, seasonal, or local), region, causes, and effects. Understanding these wind patterns is essential for meteorologists, climatologists, geographers, and policymakers involved in disaster preparedness and climate action planning.
Dashboard -:
The visualization presents wind systems in a clear table based on name, type (local, seasonal, permanent), region, cause, and effects. It supports easy comparison of global and local wind patterns. Interactive slicers let users filter by type or region, helping them focus on specific climates, analyze temporal patterns, and understand environmental impacts.

Classification and Details of Wind Systems :- 
1). Westerlies (Permanent - Mid-latitudes 30°–60°)
Cause :- Coriolis effect and pressure belts.
Effect :- Drives temperate weather systems from west to east; influences cyclonic activity.

2). Valley Winds (Local - Mountainous Areas)
Cause :- Warm air rising in valleys during the day.
Effect :- Causes upslope winds, shaping local climates and farming conditions.

3). Trade Winds (Permanent - Tropics 0°–30°)
Cause :- Earth's rotation and pressure differences.
Effect :- Regulates equatorial climate and influences ocean currents and trade routes.

4). Sirocco (Seasonal - Mediterranean)
Cause :- Hot air masses from the Sahara.
Effect :- Brings hot, dry, dusty conditions to southern Europe and North Africa.

5). Sea Breezes (Local - Coastal Regions)
Cause :- Differential heating of land and sea.
Effect :- Brings cool air inland during the day, moderating coastal temperatures.

6). Polar Easterlies (Permanent - 60°–90° Latitude)
Cause :- Cold air sinking at the poles.
Effect :- Moves cold air toward mid-latitudes, contributing to storm formation.

7). Mountain Winds (Local - Mountainous Areas)
Cause :- Cold air sinking from peaks at night.
Effect :- Produces downslope winds, cooling valley areas at night.

8). Monsoons (Seasonal - South & Southeast Asia)
Cause :- Seasonal land-sea temperature differences.
Effect :- Brings distinct wet and dry seasons, essential for agriculture.
