Comprehensive Timeline of the Israel-Palestine Conflict

Introduction -:
The Israel-Palestine conflict is one of the longest-running geopolitical disputes in modern history, involving territorial claims, religious significance, and international diplomacy. This report provides an in-depth analysis of key events, casualties, and international responses from the late 19th century to the present. The visualization offers a structured timeline of significant milestones in the conflict, providing historical context for ongoing tensions.

Major Events in the Israel-Palestine Conflict -:
1897: The Birth of Zionism
The Zionist movement advocating for a Jewish homeland begins.
First Zionist Congress held in Switzerland.
International Response: Mixed reactions; UK expresses interest.

1917: Balfour Declaration
The British government officially supports a Jewish homeland in Palestine.
Arab leaders reject the declaration, fearing displacement.
International Response: British mandate imposed after Ottoman Empire’s defeat in WWI.

1920: British Mandate Begins
League of Nations grants the UK control over Palestine.
Ottoman Empire formally dissolves.
Increased Jewish migration sparks tensions with Arab communities.

1936-1939: Arab Revolt
Palestinian Arabs stage an uprising against British rule and Zionist immigration.
Estimated 5,000 Palestinian casualties and ~500 British casualties.
British forces brutally suppress the revolt.

1947: UN Partition Plan
United Nations proposes dividing Palestine into Jewish and Arab states.
Jewish leaders accept; Arab leaders reject.
International Response: Heightened tensions leading to war.

1948: Israel’s Declaration of Independence & First Arab-Israeli War
Israel declares statehood; Arab nations invade.
Estimated 10,000+ casualties on both sides.
Outcome: Israel expands its territory beyond UN partitions; Palestinian refugees displaced.

1967: Six-Day War
Israel captures Gaza, West Bank, Sinai Peninsula, and Golan Heights.
Palestinian territories occupied; start of long-term military control.
International Response: UN Resolution 242 calls for Israeli withdrawal (not fully implemented).

1987-1993: First Intifada
Palestinian uprising against Israeli occupation.
Thousands killed and imprisoned.
International Response: Leads to the 1993 Oslo Accords, which introduce Palestinian self-governance.

2000-2005: Second Intifada
Triggered by a visit to the Al-Aqsa Mosque by Israeli leader Ariel Sharon.
Suicide bombings, Israeli military operations escalate.
Thousands of casualties; Israeli security wall constructed.

2014-Present: Ongoing Conflicts
Periodic wars between Israel and Hamas in Gaza.
Civilian casualties on both sides; international calls for peace negotiations.
Two-state solution: Remains a key but unresolved debate.

Major Global Alliances: A Comprehensive Overview

Introduction :-
Throughout history, international alliances have played a crucial role in shaping global security, economic cooperation, and political stability. These alliances serve various purposes, ranging from intelligence sharing to collective defense and regional stability. This report explores key global alliances, their founding years, objectives, and current statuses, offering a structured analysis of their impact on international relations.

Dashboard -:

- Differentiates between active and dissolved organizations.

Key Global Alliances -:
1). Five Eyes (1946 - Present)
Headquarters: No formal HQ
Member Countries: USA, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand
Objective: Intelligence-sharing network
Status: Active
Significance: Enhances security cooperation among English-speaking nations through extensive intelligence collaboration.

2). The Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance (Rio Pact) (1947 - Present)
Headquarters: Washington, D.C.
Member Countries: Latin American countries + USA
Objective: Mutual defense treaty in the Americas
Status: Active
Significance: Strengthens collective security in the Western Hemisphere against external threats.

3). NATO (1949 - Present)
Headquarters: Brussels, Belgium
Member Countries: USA, UK, Canada, Germany, France, Italy, and 27 others (32 total)
Objective: Collective defense under Article 5
Status: Active
Significance: The largest military alliance, designed to ensure the security of North America and Europe.

4). ANZUS (1951 - Present)
Headquarters: No formal HQ
Member Countries: Australia, New Zealand, USA
Objective: Pacific security cooperation
Status: Active
Significance: A strategic alliance ensuring security in the Pacific region.

5). SEATO (1954 - 1977) (Defunct)
Headquarters: Bangkok, Thailand
Member Countries: USA, UK, France, Australia, Pakistan, Thailand, etc.
Objective: Anti-communist military alliance
Status: Dissolved
Significance: Created as a countermeasure against communist expansion in Southeast Asia.

6). Warsaw Pact (1955 - 1991) (Defunct)
Headquarters: Moscow, USSR
Member Countries: Soviet Union, Poland, East Germany, Czechoslovakia, etc.
Objective: Counterbalance to NATO during the Cold War
Status: Dissolved
Significance: The Soviet Union's military response to NATO, representing Eastern Bloc defense interests.

7). NORAD (1958 - Present)
Headquarters: Colorado Springs, USA
Member Countries: USA, Canada
Objective: Aerospace warning and control for North America
Status: Active
Significance: Provides defense against airborne threats, including missile detection and response.
