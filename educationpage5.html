Student Enrollment in Brazil at Primary & Secondary Level

INTRODUCTION -:
This report examines the trends in student enrollment at primary and secondary education levels in Brazil from 2005 to 2020. It explores the changes in enrollment numbers, gross enrollment ratios (GER), and gender-specific trends.
And providing a comprehensive view of the education landscape and challenges in the education sector.

DASHBOARD -:
This Dashboard provide a clear depiction of enrollment trends for primary, lower secondary, and upper secondary education in Brazil. They illustrate shifts in GER for males and females across levels, enabling stakeholders to identify critical changes and gaps in education access. The visuals offer a clear and concise representation of growth trajectories and gender-specific trends.

Key Trends in Student Enrollment -:
1) Enrollment Numbers:
Primary Education: Enrollment declined from 18.7 million in 2005 to 15.4 million in 2020, reflecting demographic changes and transitions in the education system.
Lower Secondary Education: Enrollment decreased from 15.0 million in 2005 to 13.1 million in 2020, showing a similar pattern as primary education.
Upper Secondary Education: Enrollment showed a slight decline, moving from 9.7 million in 2005 to 9.1 million in 2020, but remained relatively stable compared to other levels.
2) Gross Enrollment Ratios (GER):
Primary Level: GER for males was 102.9%, while for females, it reached 106.7% in 2020, indicating near-universal enrollment at this level.
Lower Secondary Level: GER for males increased to 137.2%, with females reaching 133.9% in 2020, highlighting over-age enrollment due to repeating grades.
Upper Secondary Level: GER for males rose to 91.5% and females to 85.5%, reflecting ongoing efforts to expand access to higher education levels.
3) Gender Trends:
Female students consistently exhibited higher GER at the primary and lower secondary levels, while male students led at the upper secondary level.
The narrowing gender gap at all levels indicates progress toward gender parity in education.

Insights and Implications-:
1) Access to Education:
Declining enrollment numbers suggest demographic shifts and a need to focus on quality and retention. GER trends reflect efforts to ensure universal access, though challenges remain at the upper secondary level.
2)Quality and Retention:
Over-age enrollment, indicated by GER above 100%, underscores the need for interventions to improve progression rates.
3)Gender Equality:
Continued emphasis on female education is crucial for achieving equity and empowering women through education.
