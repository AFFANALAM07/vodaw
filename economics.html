Data Visualizations Related to Economics

Explore various Data Visualizations related to economic trends, policies, and global financial systems across different regions and industries. Our platform provides insightful visualizations that help you understand market dynamics, key economic indicators, and statistical analyses in the world of economics. From international trade patterns to sector-specific economic developments, we bring the data to life through visually engaging and interactive displays, making complex financial data accessible and comprehensible.

Middle East Crude Oil Production in 2023

This dashboard provides an interactive visualization of crude oil production across the Middle East. It features a slicer that enables users to filter the data by country, allowing for a focused analysis of specific Country. A heatmap visually represents the varying levels of oil production, making it easy to identify which countries contribute the most or least to the region's total output. In addition, a geographic map of the Middle East highlights the location of each country alongside its crude oil production figures. A card summarizes the overall daily crude oil production in barrels For every Country.
Note -: 1 bbl ≃ 119 Liter

State wise Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Equity Inflow

This dashboard provides an interactive visualization of Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in India. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is a key source of investment in India, involving non-residents People investing in Indian companies. This visualization showcases foreign investments across various sectors, on this Dashboard featuring tools like a State Button that allows users to filter data by state. a Total FDI Amount in Crore (Rupees) Scorecard displaying the overall FDI Amount received in Rupees, an FDI Time Chart tracking trends over the years, and a Quarter-wise Percentage Chart illustrating the distribution of Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) by quarter.

India's GDP Per Capita Income

The time-lapse chart of India's GDP Per Capita Income from 1960 to 2024 illustrates the country’s economic growth over six decades. It highlights key phases, including slow growth in the early years, a sharp rise after economic liberalization in the 1990s, and continued acceleration in the 2000s and beyond. Despite occasional setbacks, the overall trend shows India’s steady rise in per capita income, reflecting improvements in living standards and economic development.
