Sanctions on Russia: From the Cold War to the Present

Introduction -:
Sanctions have been a key geopolitical tool used by Western nations to exert economic and political pressure on Russia from the Cold War era to the present. These sanctions have targeted various sectors, including trade, energy, defense, and finance, in response to issues such as military interventions, human rights violations, and geopolitical conflicts. This report provides a detailed analysis of major sanctions imposed on Russia, their rationale, and their impact on the Russian economy and global affairs.

Dashboard -: The visualization categorizes sanctions into:
Era-Based Analysis:Displays Cold War, Post-Soviet, and Modern sanction periods.
Sectoral Impact: Highlights which industries (trade, defense, energy, banking) were most affected.
Reason for Sanctions: Identifies patterns in Western responses to Russian actions.
Slicer Functionality The slicer allows users to filter sanctions based on:
Time Period (Cold War, Post-Soviet, Modern).
Affected Sectors (Finance, Energy, Defense, Agriculture, Technology).
Imposing Entities (USA, EU, NATO, G7).

Major Conflicts Around the Globe Since World War I

Introduction -:
The 20th and 21st centuries have been marked by significant conflicts that have shaped international politics, economies, and societies. These wars and conflicts have resulted in territorial changes, ideological shifts, and large-scale human casualties. This report analyzes major conflicts from World War I onwards, highlighting their causes, key belligerents, estimated casualties, and global impact.

Dashboard -:
Overview of the Visualization
Chronological Timeline: Displays conflicts in order of occurrence, showcasing duration and historical context.
Casualty Comparisons: A visual representation of estimated casualties across different wars.
Causes and Outcomes Mapping: Highlights recurring themes such as nationalism, expansionism, and terrorism.
Slicer Functionality
The slicer enables users to filter conflicts by:
Time Period: (e.g., World War Era, Cold War Conflicts, Modern Conflicts).
Casualties: (low, medium, high impact conflicts).
Regions: (Europe, Middle East, Asia, etc.).

Key Conflicts and Their Impact -:
1). World War I (1914-1918)
Belligerents: Allies vs. Central Powers
Primary Causes: Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, militarism, alliances, imperialism, nationalism.
Outcome: Treaty of Versailles, dissolution of empires (Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, German, and Russian), Germany's defeat.
Estimated Casualties: 16 million.
Significance: Led to major geopolitical changes and set the stage for World War II.

2). World War II (1939-1945)
Belligerents: Allies vs. Axis Powers
Primary Causes: Expansionism, Treaty of Versailles grievances, rise of fascism.
Outcome: Axis Powers defeated, formation of the United Nations, start of the Cold War.
Estimated Casualties: 70 million.
Significance: The deadliest war in human history, reshaping global political alliances.

3). Yugoslav Wars (1991-2001)
Belligerents: Yugoslav Republics, NATO.
Primary Causes: Ethnic tensions, nationalism, breakup of Yugoslavia.
Outcome: Independent states formed from the former Yugoslavia.
Estimated Casualties: 140,000.
Significance: Marked the violent dissolution of Yugoslavia and redefined European borders.

4). Yom Kippur War (1973)
Belligerents: Israel vs. Egypt, Syria.
Primary Causes: Arab-Israeli conflict.
Outcome: Stalemate, diplomatic shifts.
Estimated Casualties: 30,000.
Significance: Influenced future peace agreements and reshaped Middle Eastern geopolitics.

5). War in Afghanistan (2001-2021)
Belligerents: USA, NATO vs. Taliban.
Primary Causes: 9/11 attacks, War on Terror.
Outcome: Taliban regained control in 2021.
Estimated Casualties: 240,000.
Significance: Highlighted challenges in nation-building and counterinsurgency.
