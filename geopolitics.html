Indian Population Growth

The time-lapse chart of India's population growth from 1955 to 2024 visually captures the expansion of the country's population over more than six decades. Starting in 1960, the chart shows how India's population steadily increased year after year. In the early decades, the growth rate was rapid due to high birth rates and improved healthcare. As the chart progresses, it highlights key moments in India’s demographic changes, including a period of slower growth in recent years as a result of government policies , urbanization, and declining fertility rates.

2024 Global Net Migration

This Dashboard presents the net migration status of countries in 2024. On the world map, countries experiencing more people leaving are highlighted in red, while those with more people arriving are shown in green. By using the Country Button, you can select a specific country to view its detailed migration data. The Scorecard will display the net migration value. If the background of the scorecard is red, it indicates a negative net migration, meaning more people are leaving the country. If the background is green, it indicates positive net migration, with more people moving into the country.
