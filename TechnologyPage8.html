Yearly Search Engine Usage Trends (2009-2024)

Introduction :-
Search engines are the backbone of internet accessibility, enabling users to find information efficiently. Over the years, various search engines have competed for dominance, with Google leading the industry. This report analyzes the yearly percentage of search engine usage from 2009 to 2024, highlighting trends, market shifts, and emerging competitors.
Dashboard :-
The visualization presents-:
Line Graphs: Display year-over-year market share trends of Google, Bing, and other search engines.
Market Share Comparisons: Highlights the percentage variation of search engines across different years.
Regional Search Engine Popularity: Differentiates global versus regional search engine usage trends.
Slicer Functionality:The slicer allows users to filter data based on:
Specific Years (e.g., viewing search engine trends in 2015 vs. 2023).
Search Engine Brands (e.g., isolating Google’s trends from competitors).
Regional Markets (where applicable).

Search Engine Market Trends:
1). Google’s Dominance:
2009: 89% | 2019: 92.63% | 2024: 90.81%
Google has consistently held over 89% of the market share since 2009. Its highest recorded market share was 92.63% in 2019, with a slight decline noted in 2024, indicating minor fluctuations but sustained dominance.

2). Bing’s Market Presence:
Bing’s market share ranged between 1.9% and 3.7% over the years. Its highest usage was in 2024 at 3.7%, reflecting steady but limited growth.

3). Yahoo!, Baidu, and Other Competitors:
Yahoo! maintained an average of 2.5% - 3.5% market share, while Baidu, Yandex, DuckDuckGo, and Ask Jeeves collectively remained below 3%, catering to niche user bases.

4). Minor Players and Regional Variations:
Naver and Haosou are primarily used in South Korea and China, respectively. AOL and Ask Jeeves saw significant declines over the years, almost fading from mainstream usage.

Conclusion:
Google remains the undisputed leader in search engine usage, with minor competitors maintaining limited market shares. While Bing and Yahoo! sustain steady figures, alternative search engines struggle to capture substantial global attention. The visualization enables a dynamic understanding of these trends, making it a valuable tool for market researchers and tech analysts.

Recommendations:
Competitor Growth Strategies: Bing and Yahoo! should focus on differentiation to gain more market share.
Regional Expansion: Search engines like Baidu and Yandex should optimize global reach strategies.
Consumer Awareness: Promoting privacy-focused alternatives like DuckDuckGo could shift user preferences over time.
